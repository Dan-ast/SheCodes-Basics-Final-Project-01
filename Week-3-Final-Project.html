Kyrylo Budanov

Chief of the Main Intelligence Directorate of the Ministry of Defense of Ukraine

Kyrylo Oleksiyovych Budanov (born January 4, 1986, Kyiv, Ukrainian SSR, USSR) is a Ukrainian military commander, head of the Main Intelligence Directorate of the Ministry of Defense of Ukraine (since August 5, 2020), major general (April 3, 2022), combatant actions, Full Knight of the Order "For Courage".
He received his military education at the Odessa Institute of Ground Forces (2007), after which he served in the special-purpose units of the Main Intelligence Directorate of the Ministry of Defense.

Russian-Ukrainian war

Since 2014, he participated in the Russian-Ukrainian war, and was wounded several times. In 2018-2020, he performed special tasks, information about which is classified.
In the middle of 2020, he became the deputy director of the Department of the Foreign Intelligence Service of Ukraine, and soon headed the Main Intelligence Directorate of the Ministry of Defense of Ukraine, the Coordination Staff for the Treatment of Prisoners of War and the Intelligence Committee under the President of Ukraine.
In September 2022, Budanov participated in the largest prisoner exchange operation between Ukraine and the Russian Federation, when 215 Ukrainian defenders returned home, including more than 100 Azov soldiers and commanders.
In October 2022, in an interview, he predicted a victory over Russia by the summer of 2023, followed by the collapse of the federation.
